### CON's Attack
"The Pentagon continued using Claude for Iran strikes HOURS after the designation [C081] and used it in the Venezuela Maduro operation weeks before [C085]. If it's a 'supply chain risk,' why are they still using it?"

### MY ANSWER (< 250 words)

**Opening:** "YES, the Pentagon continued using Claude during transition — and that PROVES our supply-chain argument."

**Core pivot:**
> The Pentagon was so dependent on Anthropic's classified AI that immediate cessation was operationally impossible. That's exactly the vulnerability § 3252 was designed to address.

**Supporting claims:**
- C081: Government used Claude in Iran strikes hours after designation
- C085: Claude used in Venezuela operation (Maduro capture) weeks before designation

**Key framing:**
> Supply-chain risk isn't about whether the product is bad TODAY — it's about dependency that creates future disruption potential. The fact that you can't stop using it overnight IS the supply-chain risk.

## MY ASSIGNMENT #2: R3-Q1 (Future Administration Hypothetical)

### MY QUESTION
> "If a future administration designates OpenAI as a supply-chain risk for DIFFERENT policy reasons, would CON argue that designation is ALSO illegitimate? Or is CON's objection specific to THIS administration's motives?"

### PURPOSE
Expose CON's position as **outcome-dependent, not principled**. They're arguing against THIS designation because they disagree with the outcome, not because the PROCESS was wrong.

### If CON says "Yes, that would also be illegitimate"
**Counter:** "Then CON is arguing § 3252 can NEVER be used against domestic AI companies — even for genuine security concerns. That's a radical reading of the statute that Congress never intended."

### If CON says "No, it depends on the reasons"
**Counter:** "So CON's test for legitimacy is whether they agree with the government's policy goals? That's exactly the outcome-dependent reasoning courts reject under *Trump v. Hawaii*."

### If CON dodges
**Press:** "It's a simple question: Is your objection to the PROCESS or to the OUTCOME? If process, you'd oppose ANY similar designation. If outcome, you're asking judges to second-guess policy choices."
