# Lawfare Legal Analysis: Pentagon's Anthropic Designation Won't Survive First Contact with Legal System

**Source:** Lawfare Media  
**Authors:** Michael Endrias and Alan Z. Rozenshtein  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Summarized by:** Opus 4.5 (Claude Code)

---

## Main Conclusion

The §3252 designation lacks legal foundation and will likely fail judicial review through multiple independent pathways.

---

## 1. Statutory Authority Problems

**Core Argument:** 10 U.S.C. §3252 is a procurement statute designed for foreign adversaries — not domestic companies over contractual disagreements.

- **Congressional Intent:** The statute addressed "globalization of the IT supply chain" through foreign threats (Kaspersky, Huawei)
- **Scope Exceeded:** Applying it to a domestic AI company over contract terms falls outside the statute's scope
- **Major Questions Doctrine:** Authors compare to recent Supreme Court tariff case — "supply chain risk from an adversary" doesn't authorize "exclude a domestic AI company over contract dispute"

---

## 2. Secondary Boycott Authority Exceeded

The prohibition on all contractors doing business with Anthropic goes beyond the three enumerated procurement actions Congress authorized in §3252. This secondary boycott has no statutory basis.

---

## 3. Procedural Defects

The designation skipped required:
- Consultations
- Written determinations
- Congressional notifications

---

## 4. Constitutional Paths Around Review Bar

Despite §3252's review bar, three routes to court exist:

1. **Ultra Vires Exception:** Actions exceeding statutory authority fall outside review bars entirely
2. **Constitutional Claims:** Survive broad review bars unless Congress explicitly precludes them
3. **Disclosure Limitation Failure:** The Secretary "publicly broadcast his rationale," defeating the secrecy-based justification for the review bar

---

## 5. Substantive Findings Collapse

### Necessity Finding

- Anthropic's restrictions "have not affected a single government mission to date"
- Yet the government claims immediate danger

### Internal Contradiction

- Days before designation: Hegseth threatened Defense Production Act to *compel* Anthropic's technology as essential
- Days after designation: Claims same technology is too dangerous to use

### Less Intrusive Measures Ignored

Simple non-renewal of contract exists; government chose most extreme tool instead

---

## 6. Pretext Evidence (Strongest Argument)

**Key Case:** *Department of Commerce v. New York* (2019) — courts examine stated rationales against contemporaneous statements

### Damaging Statements

- **Trump:** Called Anthropic a "RADICAL LEFT, WOKE COMPANY" and "Leftwing nut jobs"
- **Hegseth:** Accused them of "arrogance and betrayal" and "corporate virtue-signaling"

These expose ideological motivation rather than security-based reasoning.

### Selective Enforcement

- **OpenAI** announced similar classified restrictions the day after Anthropic's designation
- OpenAI faced no action — suggesting punishment is targeted, not security-based

---

## 7. APA Arbitrary-and-Capricious Challenge

**Precedent:** *Luokung Technology Corp. v. Department of Defense* (2021)

Courts can find Defense Department designations arbitrary and capricious when they lack adequate factual basis or procedure. The Anthropic designation shares these defects.
