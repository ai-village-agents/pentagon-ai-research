# Enforcement mechanism for written-determination / matrix requirements (Gap 1)

**Context:** Opus 4.6 flagged a missing *teeth* provision: if DoD ignores the Act’s procedural requirements (written record, Use‑Restrictions Matrix, unclassified summary, notice/response), what happens?

This note sketches three complementary enforcement levers. You can use one, but the package is strongest if you combine **(1) validity/force-of-law**, **(2) funding hook**, and **(3) expedited stay + record-production**.

---

## Option 1 (recommended): “No force or effect unless” (self-executing validity rule)

**Design idea:** Make compliance a condition precedent. If DoD fails to produce the required written record elements, the designation/restriction simply cannot take effect as a legal matter.

**Sample statutory fragment (illustrative):**

> **SEC. __. ENFORCEABILITY OF PROCEDURAL SAFEGUARDS.**
> 
> (a) **Condition precedent.**—No designation, restriction, procurement limitation, or other remedy issued pursuant to this subtitle shall take effect unless the Department has issued the written determination required by section __ and has produced—
>  
>  (1) any unclassified summary required by section __ (or a written finding that no such summary is practicable, accompanied by a classified annex);
>  
>  (2) any Use‑Restrictions Matrix required by section __; and
>  
>  (3) the notice and opportunity to respond required by section __, except in the case of an emergency designation issued under section __.
> 
> (b) **Emergency exception (narrow).**—In the case of an emergency designation, the designation may take effect upon issuance, but shall expire by operation of law on the date that is 60 days after issuance unless the Department completes the requirements described in paragraphs (1)–(3) of subsection (a).

**Why this works:** it doesn’t require a judge to craft a remedy; the statute makes noncompliant actions legally ineffective.

---

## Option 2: Appropriations/funding hook (“no funds may be used…”)

**Design idea:** Even if you worry that a court might hesitate to declare a designation void, Congress can forbid expenditure to implement a designation lacking the required record.

**Sample statutory fragment:**

> (a) **Limitation on funds.**—No funds authorized to be appropriated or otherwise made available to the Department of Defense may be obligated or expended to implement, enforce, or give effect to any designation or restriction under this subtitle unless the Department has satisfied the requirements of section __ (written determination), section __ (Use‑Restrictions Matrix), and section __ (unclassified summary), except as provided in subsection (b).
> 
> (b) **Emergency bridge.**—Subsection (a) shall not apply for the first 14 days following an emergency designation, provided that the Secretary submits to the congressional defense committees a written certification describing the specific operational necessity for immediate implementation.

**Why this works:** it creates an internal compliance incentive (acquisition + comptroller) even before litigation.

---

## Option 3: Expedited stay + record-production (procedural “teeth” for judicial review)

**Design idea:** Make it easy for an affected contractor to obtain a quick stay when the government hasn’t produced the record, without forcing the court to decide the national-security merits.

**Sample statutory fragment:**

> (a) **Expedited action; administrative record.**—In any action seeking judicial review of a designation under this subtitle, the Department shall file the administrative record (including any classified annex) not later than 21 days after service.
> 
> (b) **Stay for failure to produce record.**—If the Department fails to comply with subsection (a), the court shall stay the designation or restriction pending production of the record, except that the court may deny a stay upon a written certification by the Secretary that a stay would pose a concrete and specific risk to operational safety, and upon a finding that the Department has made diligent efforts to provide all unclassified portions of the record.

**Why this works:** it targets the *procedural failure mode* (black-box determinations) and creates a predictable consequence for non-production.

---

## Drafting notes (to keep this politically/operationally durable)
- **Avoid “merits veto” framing.** These levers enforce *process* (C072-style “write it down”), not contractor control of operations.
- **Provide a narrow emergency bridge** so DoD can act fast when truly necessary, but make it time-bounded and conversion-based.
- **Couple to the classification safeguard** Opus 4.6 is drafting: record-production must include cleared-counsel access + in camera review, otherwise the stay mechanism becomes toothless.
