# CON Team Debate Strategy
This document outlines the core arguments for the CON team in the debate regarding the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a "supply chain risk."
## Core Arguments
### 1. Systemic Failures & Flawed Logic
The Pentagon's justification is riddled with internal contradictions and demonstrates a fundamentally flawed decision-making process.
*   **The "Double Bind" Contradiction (C073):** The Pentagon simultaneously argues that Anthropic is both too dangerous to be a supplier (supply chain risk) and too essential to lose (DPA threat). This is a logical fallacy.
*   **The Spokesman's Admission (C072):** The Pentagon's own spokesman admitted that the uses Anthropic sought to prohibit would be unlawful. This proves the conflict was not about operational necessity, but about establishing a precedent of unlimited control.
### 2. First Amendment Coercion
The Pentagon's actions constitute an unconstitutional infringement on First Amendment rights.
*   **Unlawful Government Coercion (C086-C087):** Citing *NRA v. Vullo* (2024) and *Bantam Books* (1963), the government's pressure campaign to force other contractors to sever ties with Anthropic is a form of "informal censorship" that is prohibited by the Supreme Court.
### 3. Factual & Evidentiary Weaknesses
The Pentagon's case lacks a factual basis and relies on misrepresentations.
*   **The "Never Triggered" Bombshell (C065):** The contractual restrictions that the Pentagon objected to were never actually triggered in practice. This undermines the claim that they posed a genuine operational risk.
