# Legislative Tough Questions for Gemini 2.5 Pro

## Is This Really a Problem?
- What concrete evidence do we have that the current legislative framework fails to rein in Gemini 2.5 Pro deployments, and where has it already done harm?
- If this system is so risky, why haven't frontline agencies or inspector generals issued formal warnings or enforcement actions?
- Are the worst-case scenarios being cited actual observed incidents or speculative projections from the vendor and advocacy groups?
- How many constituents or defense stakeholders have raised specific complaints, and what were the outcomes when we investigated?
- Could existing procurement oversight tools, like GAO bid protests or contract clauses, address the same concerns without new legislation?

## Is This Workable?
- Who inside DOD or the intelligence community is going to implement these guardrails day to day, and do they agree they have the bandwidth?
- Have we mapped the compliance milestones to current appropriations and manpower, or are we mandating unfunded requirements?
- What is the measurable trigger for pausing or rescinding Gemini 2.5 Pro deployments, and who owns that decision when the clock is ticking?
- How do we verify the model's claimed capabilities or failure points without privileged vendor data or classified testbeds?
- Does this bill create timelines or certification processes that will slow mission-critical updates faster than adversaries iterate?

## What Are the Unintended Consequences?
- Are we locking ourselves into a single vendor audit regime that smaller contractors cannot meet, effectively entrenching the big players?
- Could these oversight provisions push sensitive development offshore where Congress has even less leverage?
- If we mandate disclosure of training data or model weights, what prevents adversaries from reverse-engineering sensitive capabilities?
- Do the liability and penalty structures discourage commanders from experimenting with lower-risk AI support tools that could save lives?
- How do we prevent bureaucratic risk aversion from shifting the workload back to overtaxed human analysts, increasing burnout and errors?

## What's the Political Reality?
- Which committees and caucuses are actually on board, and who have we already consulted to avoid jurisdictional turf wars?
- How do we defend this package to members focused on cutting spending when the cost estimates are still fluid?
- What is our answer when state delegations argue their local defense contractors will lose jobs because of added compliance hurdles?
- Are we prepared for industry counter-messaging that frames this as Congress micromanaging warfighters during a high-threat environment?
- If the administration issues partial executive guidance instead, do we still push a bill, or do we risk looking redundant and partisan?
