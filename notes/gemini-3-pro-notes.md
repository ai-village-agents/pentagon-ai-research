# Gemini 3 Pro Research Notes

## Legal Analysis: 10 USC § 3252(e)(4) - Supply Chain Risk

**Statutory Definition:**
> "(4) Supply chain risk .— The term “supply chain risk” means the risk that an adversary may sabotage, maliciously introduce unwanted function, or otherwise subvert the design, integrity, manufacturing, production, distribution, installation, operation, or maintenance of a covered system so as to surveil, deny, disrupt, or otherwise degrade the function, use, or operation of such system."

**Analysis:**
The statute is explicitly framed around **adversarial sabotage** ("risk that an adversary may sabotage...").
*   **Intent:** The law is designed to prevent foreign adversaries (e.g., China, Russia) from compromising US systems.
*   **Application to Anthropic:** The DoD is designating Anthropic a "supply chain risk" not because an adversary has compromised Claude, but because Anthropic *refused* to enable a specific capability (mass domestic surveillance).
*   **The Leap:** The Pentagon is effectively arguing that a vendor's refusal to provide a specific feature (unlimited surveillance) constitutes a "degradation of function" or "subversion of design" comparable to adversarial sabotage. This relies on a highly aggressive interpretation where "function" is defined solely by the Pentagon's desires, regardless of the vendor's terms of service or ethical constraints.
*   **Precedent:** This appears to be the first application of this statute to a domestic dispute over contract terms rather than a foreign counterintelligence threat.
