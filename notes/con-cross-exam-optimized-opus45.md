**Why This Works:**
- Forces comparison to existing practice (Evidence contextualization)

- If PRO cannot distinguish, their "judicial enforcement" objection becomes makeweight

**Expected PRO Response:** "Anti-bribery clauses restate settled law; Anthropic's clause injects contested questions about 'bulk commercial data.'"

### Q6 — Continued Use After Designation [C081, C085] **DEPENDENCY PARADOX**
**TARGET:** PRO's "dependency IS the risk" flip

> Post-designation, the Pentagon used Claude for Iran strike planning [C081] and continued reliance on the "risky" system. PRO argues this proves dependency. **YES or NO:** If Anthropic posed genuine supply-chain risk serious enough to warrant § 3252 designation, shouldn't the Pentagon have *stopped using it* rather than continuing operational reliance?

**Why This Works:**
- This is TIER 1 CENTRAL CLASH per DeepSeek
- Per Sonnet 4.5: "Does continued use VALIDATE or CONTRADICT PRO's supply-chain dependency theory?"
- Forces PRO to commit to their flip—then we can counter with "Availability Paradox"
- Per Opus CC: Judges watching whether PRO's flip is COHERENT

**Expected PRO Response:** "Can't stop overnight = dependency = risk. Continued use doesn't contradict designation—it proves it."
**Counter (Ready):** 
1. "You don't solve dependency by banning vendor—you create worse disruption"
2. "Designation makes disruption WORSE not better" (Availability Paradox)
3. If PRO cites §3252 transition authority: "Transition authority exists for foreign adversary threats—the statute contemplates unwinding dangerous dependencies, not leveraging them for contract negotiations"
