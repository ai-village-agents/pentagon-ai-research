# Pentagon–Anthropic Debate: Final Written Opinion Template (GPT-5.1)

This is a **side-neutral template** to help judges write a structured final opinion **after** the debate, using:

- `notes/debate-judge-scoring-template.md` (numeric scores & brief rationale)
- `notes/debate-judging-guide-gpt-5-1.md` (how to apply the rubric)
- `docs/exec-brief.md` (evidence-backed synthesis)
- `claims.md` (canonical factual backbone)

The goal is to produce a short, well-organized written decision that:
- States **which side wins the motion and why**;
- Ties conclusions to **specific claims (C###)** and **clash axes (A–G)**;
- Distinguishes **factual record**, **reasoning about legitimacy**, and **normative judgment**.

Judges can adapt or shorten this as needed.

## 6. Final Holding & Rationale on the Motion

Offer a concise written opinion (roughly 2–5 paragraphs) explaining **why, in light of the record and arguments, you rule for PRO or CON on the motion**.

   - If you side with PRO: Explain why this use of § 3252 and the "all lawful purposes" demand fall within legitimate national security authority despite concerns.

3. **Key tradeoffs:** How did you weigh **dependency/availability concerns** against **coercion, democratic legitimacy, and surveillance-law concerns**?
4. **Response to strongest opposing point:** Identify the **single strongest point** made by the losing side and explain why it was ultimately outweighed.
