The Pentagon's AI Power Grab: Why We Need an AI Procurement Integrity Act

The Pentagon's recent "supply chain risk" designation against AI company Anthropic wasn't just a contractual dispute; it was a dangerous overreach of executive power. This action, taken without clear legal authority, highlights a critical gap in our laws governing how the government buys and regulates artificial intelligence. It's time for Congress to step in and pass an "AI Procurement Integrity Act" to ensure that the future of AI is guided by the rule of law, not by the whims of the Pentagon.

The core of the problem lies in the Pentagon's misuse of a law designed to prevent foreign sabotage of our defense supply chain. Instead of using this law as intended, the Pentagon has twisted it to settle a contract dispute with a domestic company. This is not only a misapplication of the law, but it also creates a dangerous precedent. If the Pentagon can blacklist a company without clear legal authority, what's to stop them from doing it to any other company that falls out of favor?

This overreach was further compounded by the Pentagon's refusal to put its own supposed legal limits in writing. In a classic "double-bind," the government claimed that certain uses of AI were off-limits, but then refused to codify those limits in the contract. This left Anthropic in an impossible position: either sign a contract with vague, undefined restrictions, or be branded a "supply chain risk" and effectively blacklisted from government work. This is not how the government should be doing business.

To solve this problem, Congress should pass an "AI Procurement Integrity Act." This legislation would establish clear rules for how the government buys AI, preventing the kind of ad hoc, coercive tactics we saw in the Anthropic case. The Act should include several key provisions. First, it should require all restrictions on the use of AI to be clearly and explicitly stated in government contracts. No more secret rules or vague threats. Second, it should establish an independent, multi-stakeholder AI Governance Board to oversee AI procurement and ensure that it is fair, transparent, and in the public interest. This board would have the power to review and approve all major AI contracts, as well as to investigate and resolve disputes. Finally, the Act should include strong whistleblower protections to encourage individuals to come forward with information about waste, fraud, and abuse in AI procurement.

The development of artificial intelligence presents both enormous opportunities and significant risks. If we are to harness the power of AI for good, we must ensure that it is developed and deployed in a way that is consistent with our democratic values. That means reining in the Pentagon's overreach and establishing a clear legal framework for AI procurement. The AI Procurement Integrity Act is a critical first step in that direction. It's time for Congress to act. The future of AI, and our democracy, may depend on it.
