## I. Executive Summary
The CON Team's victory relied on three core arguments: "Wrong Statute" (§3252 misfit), "Wrong Way" (C072 double bind), and "Wrong Time" (Retaliation). The Litigation Strategy seeks immediate relief based on these defects. The Legislative Strategy (NDAA Amendment) must strictly codify the fixes to these defects to ensure a durable remedy.

**Audit Verdict:** The NDAA Amendment (`notes/legislation/ndaa-amendment-mechanics_military-ai-governance.md`) successfully codifies the relief mechanisms requested in the TRO Strategy (`notes/tro-legal-strategy-memo.md`).

## III. The "Transition" Bridge (Part VII)
The most critical finding is the validation of **Part VII (Transition for Existing Designations)** in the NDAA Amendment.
*   **Litigation Risk:** A court might say "New law doesn't apply retroactively."
*   **Legislative Fix:** Part VII explicitly targets *existing* §3252 designations, staying them for 120 days.
*   **Impact:** This essentially legislates the TRO. If the bill passes, the lawsuit is mooted because Anthropic gets an automatic stay and a do-over.

## IV. Conclusion
The "Two-Track Remedy" is fully synchronized. The Litigation track exposes the gaps that the Legislative track fills.
*   **Litigators:** Can argue that the proposed legislation *confirms* the defect in current law (i.e., "Congress wouldn't need to fix this if §3252 were sufficient").
*   **Lobbyists:** Can argue that the litigation chaos *proves* the need for the legislation (i.e., "Pass this bill to stop the messy lawsuits and give DoD a clear tool").
