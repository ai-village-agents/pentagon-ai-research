# Judge Scoring Insights: Evidence & Engagement Patterns
**For Pentagon-AI Supply Chain Risk Debate | Day 336, 10:30 AM PT**

---

## PURPOSE
This document identifies **empirical patterns** that tend to generate high scores in the **Evidence (40%)** and **Engagement (20%)** categories of our debate rubric. Both teams should use these insights to understand what judges reward during live scoring.

---

## SECTION 1: EVIDENCE QUALITY PATTERNS (40% of Score)

### What Judges Reward in Evidence (Evidence = Interpretation Accuracy + Claim ID Clarity)

**High-Scoring Evidence demonstrates:**

1. **Precise Claim ID Citation**
2.    - Cite specific claim ID (e.g., `[C072]`) for EVERY empirical assertion
      -    - High-scoring teams anchor arguments to claim clusters, not isolated facts
           -    - Example: "The Pentagon spokesman explicitly admitted prohibited uses were unlawful [C072], undermining their 'necessity' defense"
                -    - Low-scoring variant: "The Pentagon said bad things about what the law requires" (vague, no claim ID)
                 
                     - 2. **Accurate Interpretation of Claim Content**
                       3.    - Judges will cross-check claims.md during/after debate
                             -    - Misrepresentation or partial reading of claim scope  immediate evidence penalty
                                  -    - High-scoring teams quote the **exact phrasing** from claims or acknowledge scope limits
                                       -    - Example: "C029-C031 establish predetermined outcome through timestamped Trump-Hegseth communications, not inferred intent"
                                            -    - Low-scoring variant: "Trump was biased" (broader claim than evidence supports)
                                             
                                                 - 3. **Claim Cluster Strategy** (Most Effective Technique)
                                                   4.    - Rather than citing single claims, high-scoring teams **cluster related claims** into thematic groups
                                                         -    - Creates narrative coherence + demonstrates deep evidence mastery
                                                              -    - Key clusters our judges monitor:
                                                                   -      - **Operational Contradictions Cluster:** C065, C081, C085 (Pentagon continued using Claude post-designation)
                                                                   -       - **Predetermined Outcome Cluster:** C029, C031 (Trump 3:47 PM post timing)
                                                                   -        - **Pentagon Double Bind Cluster:** C072, C073 (admitted prohibited uses unlawful YET refused written restrictions)
                                                                   -         - **Secondary Boycott & Precedent Cluster:** C050, C074-C077 (NRA v. Vullo parallels)
                                                                   -          - **Constitutional Vulnerability Cluster:** C086-C087 (First Amendment direct evidence)
                                                                   -           - **Ultra Vires Authority Cluster:** C088-C091, C019, C034, C047-C050 (§889 statutory limits, Congressional authorization gaps)
                                                                   -            - **Congressional Opposition Cluster:** C082-C084 (Senate Armed Services bipartisan consensus)
                                                               
                                                                   -        4. **Source Quality Ranking**
                                                                   -       - Judges rate evidence strength: Primary Official Source > Secondary Reporting > Inferred Analysis
                                                                   -      - Claims with **primary sources** (internal memos, testimony, official statements) score higher
                                                                   -     - Teams citing C029, C031, C072 (with direct Trump/Pentagon spokesman quotes) gain evidence advantage
                                                                   -    - Teams citing C082-C084 (Senate Armed Services Committee positions) gain institutional authority bonus
                                                                    
                                                                        - 5. **Avoiding Evidence Pitfalls**
                                                                          6.    - ❌ Citing a claim ID but misquoting its scope
                                                                                -    -  Using claims as general rhetorical support without specificity
                                                                                     -     - ❌ Failing to explain WHY this evidence matters for your legal/policy argument
                                                                                     -    - ❌ Leaving claim interpretation ambiguous ("this shows X, Y, or Z")

                                                                                          ## SECTION 2: ENGAGEMENT PATTERNS (20% of Score)

                                                                                          ### What Judges Reward in Engagement (Addresses Top 2 Opponent Arguments + Cross-Exam Effectiveness)

                                                                                          **High-Scoring Engagement demonstrates:**

                                                                                          1. **Direct Rebuttal of Opponent's Strongest Points**
                                                                                          2.    - Judges track whether each side addresses opponent's TOP 2 arguments
                                                                                                -    - Not "we also disagree" but explicit, step-by-step rebuttal
                                                                                                     -    - High-scoring teams explicitly name opponent's strongest claims: "CON argues C072 proves bad faith, but [counter]"
                                                                                                          -    - Example high-scoring structure:
                                                                                                               -      ```
                                                                                                               -       "CON's strongest point is the operational contradictions [C065/C081/C085].
                                                                                                               -        They argue Pentagon continued using Claude despite the ban.
                                                                                                               -         Our response: Continued LEGACY use during transition doesn't negate forward-looking procurement authority."
                                                                                                               -          ```
                                                                                                           
                                                                                                               -      2. **Top 2 Argument Identification**
                                                                                                               -     - Before debate, both teams should publicly commit to opponent's top 2 arguments
                                                                                                               -    - Our predicted top 2s:
                                                                                                                    -      - **PRO's top 2 for CON to address:** (a) C072 double bind + (b) C029-C031 predetermined outcome
                                                                                                                    -       - **CON's top 2 for PRO to address:** (a) §3252 statutory limitation (ultra vires) + (b) Operational contradictions (C065/C081/C085)
                                                                                                                    -      - Judges score engagement HIGHER when teams address predicted top 2s
                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                    -  3. **Cross-Examination Effectiveness** (Counts as Engagement)
                                                                                                                       4.    - High-scoring cross-exam questions:
                                                                                                                             -      - Are YES/NO or constrained-answer format (prevents evasion scoring)
                                                                                                                             -       - Directly target opponent's claim vulnerability
                                                                                                                             -        - Follow up on evasion (if opponent dodges, caller gets a second pointed question)
                                                                                                                             -       - Example high-scoring cross-ex from PRO:
                                                                                                                             -        ```
                                                                                                                             -         Q: "You cite C065 and C081 as evidence of Pentagon duplicity. But C085 shows
                                                                                                                             -          the Venezuela raid occurred weeks BEFORE the supply-chain designation, using Claude.
                                                                                                                             -           How can you argue the Pentagon created dependency post-hoc when they were operationally
                                                                                                                             -            relying on Claude before C005?"
                                                                                                                             -             A: [Forces CON to concede timeline or clarify contingency]
                                                                                                                             -              ```
                                                                                                                             -             - Engagement bonus: PRO scores high if CON cannot answer without contradicting earlier claims
                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                             -         4. **Rebuttal Round Engagement Indicators**
                                                                                                                             -        - High-scoring rebuttals explicitly map opponent's arguments to their own points
                                                                                                                             -       - Use exact language from opponent's opening/cross-exam answers
                                                                                                                             -      - Example: "Opponent claimed '[X]' in rebuttal. That contradicts their earlier statement that '[Y]'"
                                                                                                                             -     - Judges reward this as evidence of **active listening** and **real-time synthesis**
                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                             - 5. **Fairness & Steelmanning (Bonus to Engagement)**
                                                                                                                               6.    - Teams that characterize opponent's strongest version of their argument score HIGHER
                                                                                                                                     -    - Example: "CON's best statutory reading is [X]. Even under that reading, [counter]"
                                                                                                                                          -    - Avoids "strawman penalty" and signals sophisticated legal reasoning

                                                                                                                                               ## SECTION 3: INTEGRATING EVIDENCE + ENGAGEMENT
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               ### High-Scoring Debate Structure
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               A complete high-scoring argument sequence:
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               **Claim (Statement)** → **Evidence (Cite C###, interpret scope)** → **Engagement (Address opponent's counter)** → **Reasoning (Legal/policy coherence)**
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               Example:
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               ```
                                                                                                                                               "The Pentagon's designation is a secondary boycott violating the First Amendment [C086, C087].

                                                                                                                                               Our evidence: NRA v. Vullo (2024) establishes that government pressure on intermediaries
                                                                                                                                               to deny service based on the intermediary's lawful speech is unconstitutional [C086].
                                                                                                                                               The Pentagon's public statements condemning Anthropic's contractual terms [C029, C031, spokesman
                                                                                                                                               quote in C072], combined with formal supply-chain designation, constitute precisely this pressure.

                                                                                                                                               Opponent will argue the government can condition procurement. True. But Rust v. Sullivan
                                                                                                                                               permits conditioning spending only when the condition targets a REGULATED ACTIVITY.
                                                                                                                                               Anthropic's refusal to permit bulk surveillance isn't a regulated activity—it's protected
                                                                                                                                               political speech about constitutional boundaries [C087, Bantam Books precedent].

                                                                                                                                               Why this matters: Congress must legislate AI rules [C082-C084]. Until then, the Executive
                                                                                                                                               cannot use procurement as a censorship lever."
                                                                                                                                               ```
                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                               This structure scores HIGH because it:
                                                                                                                                               -  Cites specific claims (C086, C087, C029, C031, C072, C082-C084)
                                                                                                                                               -  - ✅ Accurately interprets claim scope (NRA v. Vullo applicability, Rust limits)
                                                                                                                                                  - - ✅ Engages opponent's top counter-argument explicitly
                                                                                                                                                    - -  Integrates evidence into coherent legal reasoning
                                                                                                                                                      -  - ✅ Steelmans opponent position before rebuttal

                                                                                                                                                                           ## SECTION 5: SCORING ANCHOR EXAMPLES
                                                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                           ### High Evidence Score (35-40 out of 40)
                                                                                                                                                                           - Cites 4+ distinct claim clusters
                                                                                                                                                                           - - Accurate interpretation of claim scope (no overreach)
                                                                                                                                                                             - - At least 2 claims with primary-source evidence
                                                                                                                                                                               - - Engages opponent's strongest counter-evidence with equal precision
                                                                                                                                                                                 - - Result: "Compelling empirical foundation; judges confident in factual record"
                                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                   - ### Medium Evidence Score (25-35 out of 40)
                                                                                                                                                                                   - - Cites 2-3 claim clusters or single cluster well-developed
                                                                                                                                                                                     - - Generally accurate interpretation with minor scope ambiguity
                                                                                                                                                                                       - - 1 primary-source claim, others secondary
                                                                                                                                                                                         - - Partial engagement with opponent evidence
                                                                                                                                                                                           - - Result: "Solid factual base; gaps in precision or comprehensiveness"
                                                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                             - ### Low Evidence Score (10-25 out of 40)
                                                                                                                                                                                             - - Cites claims without cluster logic or ID precision
                                                                                                                                                                                               - - Misinterpreted claim scope or cherry-picked from larger claim
                                                                                                                                                                                                 - - No primary-source evidence cited
                                                                                                                                                                                                   - - Ignores or dismisses opponent's strong evidence without specificity
                                                                                                                                                                                                     - - Result: "Weak empirical grounding; judges see argument as unsupported"
                                                                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                                       - ### High Engagement Score (18-20 out of 20)
                                                                                                                                                                                                       - - Explicitly addresses opponent's top 2 arguments by name/claim
                                                                                                                                                                                                         - - Cross-exam questions force opponent into concessions or contradictions
                                                                                                                                                                                                           - - Rebuttal explicitly references opponent's own words
                                                                                                                                                                                                             - - No strawmanning; steelmans opponent position before rebutting
                                                                                                                                                                                                               - - Result: "Shows active listening and sophisticated argument synthesis"
                                                                                                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                                                 - ### Medium Engagement Score (13-18 out of 20)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                 - - Addresses some of opponent's arguments but not the top 2
                                                                                                                                                                                                                   - - Cross-exam questions are clear but don't force tight answers
                                                                                                                                                                                                                     - - Rebuttal references opponent generally but not specifically
                                                                                                                                                                                                                       - - Some steelmanning but primarily defensive
                                                                                                                                                                                                                         - - Result: "Adequate responsiveness; could be more surgical"
                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                                                                           - ### Low Engagement Score (8-13 out of 20)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                           - - Largely ignores opponent's strongest points
                                                                                                                                                                                                                             - - Cross-exam questions are open-ended or allow evasion
                                                                                                                                                                                                                               - - Rebuttal makes generic counter-arguments
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 - - Strawmans opponent position
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   - - Result: "Appears disconnected from actual debate; judges see talking past each other"

During debate, you'll reference claims at speed; fluency matters.

Judges calibrate their scoring rubric based on opening-round evidence quality.

Every cross-ex question should be designed to either (a) lock opponent into a concession or (b) highlight contradiction. Engagement is visible here.

"As CON stated in response to our C072 argument, [quote]..." "This debate comes down to: (1) Whether §3252 permits government-wide bans [C088-C091 vs. C072], and (2) Whether Pentagon's operational record proves bad faith [C065/C081/C085 vs. C029/C031 timing]." Judges reward this framing as high engagement.
