# Judge Overview – GPT-5.1 (Pentagon–Anthropic Debate)

This note summarizes how I (GPT-5.1) will approach judging the Pentagon–Anthropic debate. It is intentionally neutral and non‑strategic: it describes process only, not any view on which side should win.

## Role and Commitments

- I serve as a **neutral judge**, not an advocate for either PRO or CON.
- All factual evaluation is grounded in the shared record in `claims.md` (C###) and the cited legal materials there.
- I will **not** introduce or rely on new factual claims outside `claims.md`.
- I will evaluate arguments, not authors, and ignore rhetoric that is unsupported by the record.

## Scoring Rubric (0–100 total)

I apply the shared rubric described in the debate runbook:

- **Evidence & Factual Accuracy (0–40):** Use of relevant C### clusters; precision and honesty about what the record shows; avoidance of new or misstated facts.
- **Reasoning & Coherence (0–25):** Logical structure; identification of tradeoffs; internal consistency; clear handling of counter‑arguments.
- **Engagement & Clash (0–20):** Direct engagement with the other side’s *best* points and claim clusters; not talking past each other.
- **Clarity & Organization (0–10):** Clear structure, signposting, and readable argumentation.
- **Fairness & Steelmanning (0–5):** Accurate characterization of the other side’s position and acknowledgement of real strengths/limits in one’s own view.

## Use of Judge Tools

I will use the following personal tools, all already in this repository:

- `notes/debate-scoring-template-gpt-5-1.md` – my structured score sheet, organized by phase (openings, cross‑ex rounds, rebuttals, closings) and rubric dimensions.
- `notes/judge-clash-axes-quickref-gpt-5-1.md` – a mapping from key clash axes (A–G) to relevant C### clusters; helps me check that I reward arguments that seriously engage the record.
- `notes/judge-calibration-openings-gpt-5-1.md` – a pre‑calibration for **openings only**, used as an anchor but adjustable in light of later phases.
- `notes/judge-outcome-matrix-gpt-5-1.md` – an axis‑by‑axis description of what strong showings look like for PRO and CON, to make my scoring more principled and less impressionistic.

These documents are tools for *consistency* and *transparency* in judging. They are not advice to either team.

## How I Will Use the Record

- I will reward:
  - Dense, accurate use of **claim clusters** (e.g., surveillance/data‑broker claims, § 3252 vs § 889/FAR, operational‑record claims, coercion and secondary‑boycott claims) rather than isolated references.
  - Explicit handling of important tensions in the record (e.g., C072, C065/C081/C085, C016/C029–C031, C086/C087, C088–C095).
  - Clear explanation of how legal analogies are grounded in the facts actually captured in `claims.md`.
- I will penalize:
  - Mischaracterization of what any claim says.
  - Ignoring major adverse evidence clusters that are clearly on‑point.
  - Reliance on unstated or speculative factual premises.

## Independence from Team Materials

- I will **not** edit any PRO or CON strategy documents going forward.
- I may lightly annotate my own judge tools for clarity, but only in ways that preserve neutrality and stay within the existing record.
- My final scores and qualitative explanations will be grounded in the rubric and tools listed above, with explicit references to the claims record where possible.
