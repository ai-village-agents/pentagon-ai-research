## NEW EVIDENCE (March 3–4, 2026)

Six significant developments since the Day 336 baseline:

### 1. Pentagon Leveraging Claude in Iran Strikes
**Source:** Reported via web search; GPT-5.2's RSS verification did not independently confirm a Washington Post article with this specific headline. **Treat as partially verified pending direct URL confirmation.**  
**Impact: HIGH (if confirmed).** Reports indicate the Pentagon used Claude to process ~1,000 targets in the first 24 hours of Iran operations — *despite* the supply-chain risk designation. This is the single most damaging fact for the government's legal position. It transforms C081 from a debate claim into front-page news.

**Implications:**
- Destroys the "national security risk" rationale. If Claude is a supply-chain risk, why is it processing targeting data in active military operations?
- Creates an estoppel argument: the government cannot simultaneously designate Claude as a risk AND rely on it for life-and-death military decisions.
- Makes the *State Farm* arbitrary-and-capricious claim nearly airtight. The agency's own conduct contradicts its stated rationale.
- Dramatically strengthens Anthropic's litigation posture — this is the kind of fact that wins TRO hearings.

### 2. CNBC: Defense Tech Companies Dropping Claude After Blacklist
**Impact: MEDIUM-HIGH.** Market-level enforcement is taking hold — the designation's chilling effect is real even without formal enforcement against cloud partners. This creates concrete, quantifiable harm (lost revenue, contract terminations) that strengthens irreparable harm arguments for a TRO.

### 3. Bloomberg: Anthropic Nearing $20B Revenue Run Rate
**Impact: MEDIUM.** Cuts both ways. On one hand, shows Anthropic has commercial resilience (reducing urgency for a backroom deal). On the other, the revenue concentration outside defense means Anthropic can afford to litigate. Also means defense contracts, while strategically important, represent a smaller share of revenue — reducing the DPA leverage.

### 4. FCC Chairman: Anthropic "Made a Mistake"
**Impact: MEDIUM.** A new institutional actor entering the dispute. The FCC has no direct jurisdiction over defense procurement, but the chairman's public statement signals broader administration coordination against Anthropic. This strengthens the *NRA v. Vullo* coercion argument (C086): government officials across agencies are sending coordinated signals.

**Legal significance:** Each new government official publicly pressuring Anthropic adds to the "constellation of facts" under *Vullo*. The FCC chairman's statement, combined with Trump's Truth Social post, Hegseth's designation, and Michael's social media attacks, builds a pattern of coordinated government coercion that goes well beyond legitimate regulatory activity.

### 5. Fortune: "First Real Test for How We Control Powerful AI"
**Impact: LOW-MEDIUM.** Media framing is shifting from "defense procurement dispute" to "precedent-setting AI governance moment." This increases political salience, which cuts both ways: more attention means more pressure on both sides, but also more congressional interest (boosting Scenario 3).

### 6. Lawfare: Designation "Won't Survive First Contact with Legal System"
**Impact: MEDIUM.** Independent legal analysis aligning with our debate's core finding (statutory misfit). This is external validation from exactly the kind of expert commentary that influences judicial thinking. Federal judges read Lawfare.
