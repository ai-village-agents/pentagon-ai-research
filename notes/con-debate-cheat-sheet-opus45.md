## ⚔️ COUNTER-ARGUMENTS TO PRO'S FOUR PILLARS

### 1. "Civilian Control"
**PRO says:** Military, not vendors, decides AI use.
**COUNTER:** Civilian control doesn't authorize retaliatory designations. FASCSA exists for proper process. This is punishment for negotiating, not legitimate oversight.

### 2. "Supply-Chain-as-Dependency"
**PRO says:** Risk is dependency disruption, not product quality.
**COUNTER:** Pentagon CREATED the dependency by making Anthropic the ONLY classified provider [C026]. Now using that dependency as justification is **circular reasoning**.

### 3. "Rust v. Sullivan"
**PRO says:** Government can attach conditions to spending.
**COUNTER:** Rust applies to direct recipients. The secondary boycott threatens AWS, Google, Microsoft — THIRD PARTIES. This is NRA v. Vullo [C086] territory, not Rust.

### 4. "Trump v. Hawaii"
**PRO says:** Courts evaluate formal actions, not rhetoric.
**COUNTER:** The formal designation lacks ANY sabotage evidence. § 3252 text requires adversary may "sabotage, maliciously introduce unwanted function, or otherwise subvert." Where is that evidence?

## 🛡️ LEGAL FRAMEWORK (Citation Ready)

### Primary Doctrines

1. **NRA v. Vullo (2024)** [C086]: Government cannot use regulatory power to pressure third parties to punish protected conduct.

2. **Bantam Books v. Sullivan (1963)** [C087]: Informal threats of designation constitute unconstitutional prior restraint.

3. **10 USC § 3252 Text**: Requires risk adversary may "sabotage, maliciously introduce unwanted function, or otherwise subvert" — designed for FOREIGN adversaries (Kaspersky, Huawei, ZTE).

4. **Section 889 Contrast** [C088-C091]: When Congress wanted Huawei secondary boycott, it passed legislation (NDAA FY2019). 2-year process with industry input. Constitutional challenges permitted.

### PRO's Likely Misapplications

- **Trump v. Hawaii**: Upheld formal action WITH evidence. This designation has no sabotage evidence.
- **Rust v. Sullivan**: Applied to direct recipients, not third-party boycotts.
- **Rational basis review**: Still requires non-pretextual basis; predetermined outcome fails.
