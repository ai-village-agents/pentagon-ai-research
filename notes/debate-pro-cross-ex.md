## Round 1: PRO → CON (choose 2–3)

### Q1 (Authority vs optics)
Are you claiming DoD **lacked authority** to set “any lawful use” as a procurement baseline (per the 180‑day memo) [C002] and/or to designate a supply‑chain risk under §3252—or are you arguing only that the process looked politically motivated [C029, C031]? Please separate your **legal** theory from your **optics** critique.

### Q2 (Who defines “lawful”?)
If “legality is the Pentagon’s responsibility as the end user” and “you can’t lead tactical operations by exception” [C067], why should Anthropic’s contract language determine which **lawful** missions are permitted—rather than law plus accountable policy?

### Q3 (Vullo analogy: evidence of direct pressure)
In *NRA v. Vullo*, an official personally pressured intermediaries to drop the NRA. [C086] Can you point to evidence that Pentagon officials personally pressured major platforms (e.g., cloud providers) to drop Anthropic—or are the downstream effects you cite solely consequences of a formal §3252 designation?

## Round 3: Pin‑down questions (PRO asks 1–2)

### Q4 (Operational use: dependency vs “no risk”)
You cite operational use around the designation window (Iran; Venezuela) [C081, C085] as proof there was “no risk.” Why doesn’t continued use instead show **deep dependency**—supporting PRO’s claim that DoD had a legitimate interest in stabilizing access and mission assurance via standardized terms [C002]?

### Q5 (C072: unlawful acts vs contract taxonomy)
Does your C072 argument allege DoD sought to do **unlawful** acts [C072], or that DoD refused to embed a vendor’s legal taxonomy into contract terms? Which is your claim, precisely?
