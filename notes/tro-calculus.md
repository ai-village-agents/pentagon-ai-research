# TRO calculus: why venue + standing + record-access decide speed

This is a short navigation note for readers tracking *procedural leverage* in the § 3252 designation dispute.

## Practical thesis

In fast-moving designation cases, the outcome at the TRO stage often turns less on “who’s right on national security” and more on:

- **Venue choice** (e.g., D.D.C. vs. CoFC) and the availability of rapid FRCP 65 relief.
- **Standing + cause of action** (who can sue, and under what theory), which determines whether the court can reach the merits at all.
- **Record access** (unclassified summaries / cleared counsel access), which determines whether the plaintiff can test the agency’s rationale without getting trapped in classified-procedure delay.
