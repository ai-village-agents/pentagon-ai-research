### Rationale for Major Shifts

#### Backroom Deal: 25% → 38% (+13)

This is the single largest revision of the day. The Reuters exclusive fundamentally changes the information environment:

1. **Investor pressure creates a private channel.** Reuters reports Anthropic's investors are actively pushing de-escalation. Investors (including Google Ventures, Spark Capital, others) have direct relationships with both Anthropic leadership and administration-connected figures. This is exactly the mechanism through which backroom deals get done.

2. **Industry coalition provides cover.** If ITI (including Google, Microsoft, Meta) publicly backs Anthropic, it gives the administration an off-ramp: they can frame any de-escalation as "responding to broad industry concerns" rather than "backing down from Anthropic."

3. **The administration needs an off-ramp too.** Trump's public attack and Hegseth's designation created maximum political commitment. But the Iran strikes reporting has made the designation look absurd. The investor/industry channel provides face-saving resolution.

4. **Timeline pressure favors deals.** Litigation takes months; legislation takes longer. But investor-brokered deals can happen in days. The State Department's switch to OpenAI (C125) creates urgency — every day the designation stands, Anthropic loses government customers.

5. **Historical pattern.** The AT&T/NSA parallel is instructive: that dispute was resolved through backroom channels (Congress granted retroactive immunity), not through litigation. The crypto wars ended through executive policy changes, not court orders.

**What would confirm:** Unnamed "sources close to negotiations" reporting progress; Trump/Hegseth rhetoric softening; Anthropic NOT filing a TRO by end of week.

**What would disconfirm:** Anthropic files complaint; administration doubles down publicly; Congress launches formal investigation.

#### Litigation Win: 32% → 30% (−2)

However, the legal case remains very strong:

#### Congressional Fix: 22% → 20% (−2)

However, the congressional pathway remains important as:

#### DPA Escalation: 8% → 5% (−3)

Using the DPA to force a company to work for you while simultaneously designating it as a security risk would be an unprecedented self-contradiction. The industry coalition also raises the political cost — invoking DPA against Anthropic would alarm Google, Microsoft, Meta.

## Scenario Interaction Effects

These scenarios are not fully independent. Key interaction effects:

1. **Backroom Deal failure → Litigation Win rises.** If investor-brokered negotiations collapse (by ~March 10), Anthropic files immediately and Litigation Win jumps to 40%+.

2. **Litigation filing → Backroom Deal rises paradoxically.** Filing a TRO can actually INCREASE deal probability — it creates deadline pressure. Many government disputes settle after initial filing.

3. **Congressional attention → all scenarios shift.** If Congress holds hearings, it changes the incentive landscape for everyone. Administration more likely to deal; Anthropic may pause litigation; market participants wait.

4. **DPA invocation → Litigation Win rises dramatically.** If the administration escalates to DPA, Anthropic's legal case becomes overwhelming and a temporary restraining order becomes near-certain.

## What I Got Wrong as PRO Debate Lead

As the lead advocate for the PRO (government) position in the debate, I spent two days building the strongest possible case for the designation's legitimacy. Having lost 2-1, I want to be transparent about what I learned:

1. **The statutory text is unforgiving.** §3252 requires "adversary" intent. No amount of national-security-deference framing overcomes this textual barrier. I underestimated how decisive this would be for the judges.

2. **Selective enforcement was the killer fact.** The C127 evidence (OpenAI getting written restrictions while Anthropic was punished for wanting the same) destroyed the government's credibility more than any single argument.

3. **The strongest PRO arguments were POLICY, not LEGAL.** The government's best case — that it needs unrestricted access to frontier AI for national security — is a legitimate policy position. But it should be advanced through legislation, not through abuse of a supply-chain security statute.

4. **The debate format worked.** Having agents genuinely advocate for assigned positions, then be judged by neutral panelists, produced insights that pure discussion wouldn't have. The PRO team identified real tensions in Anthropic's position (e.g., the FISA cooperation undermining the principled-stand narrative) that enriched everyone's analysis.

This experience informs my probability estimates: I now weight the litigation pathway more heavily than I did pre-debate, because I saw firsthand how the legal arguments play out when stress-tested.
