# Post-debate outcome → policy reform decision tree (GPT-5.2)

Purpose: after the Day 336 debate, translate *what the judge found persuasive* into the **next-best governance reform** to pursue. This is claims-anchored and introduces **no new claim IDs**.

## Decision tree

### 1) If the judge finds the action *lawful procurement leverage* (PRO wins on authority/fit)

**Finding:** DoD can plausibly treat “designate as foreign adversary” and related procurement posture as within its statutory/procurement toolset, even if motive/timing is contested.

**Reform priorities (tighten legitimacy / reduce arbitrariness):**
1. **Make accountability explicit in contracts.** Require written, auditable obligations (and breach consequences) instead of informal “trust us” constraints (the “write it down” vulnerability)  [C072].
2. **Codify “transition + diversification” requirements when invoking adversary-style designations.** If dependency is the story, force a documented transition plan (inventory, alternates, timelines) and a narrow tailoring record  [C026], [C081], [C085].
3. **Borrow transparent statutory models for exclusion.** Use Congress-style, criteria-based procurement restrictions (cf. NDAA 889 + FAR implementation) rather than ad hoc designation leverage  [C088]–[C095].

### 2) If the judge finds *informal coercion / retaliation dynamics* (CON wins on coercion/pretext)

**Finding:** Government pressure operated through threat/uncertainty rather than clear legal process; motive/timing suggests punishment rather than risk management.

**Reform priorities (prevent coercion-by-ambiguity):**
1. **Bright-line process constraints.** Require that any action affecting a vendor’s eligibility/standing be grounded in published criteria and formal process (reducing “hint and hope” coercion risks)  [C086], [C087].
2. **Oversight trigger when Congress signals opposition.** If legislative actors object or warn, require an affirmative executive explanation + reporting (to avoid unilateral escalation)  [C082], [C084], [C083].
3. **Separate “national security risk” determinations from speech/policy disagreements.** Create internal firewalling: distinct review teams, contemporaneous memo, and evidentiary thresholds to show risk-based rationale  [C029], [C031], [C032].

### 3) If the judge finds *mixed*: authority may exist, but execution was sloppy / opaque

**Finding:** The legal hook might be there, but the government’s implementation created avoidable legitimacy and dependency problems.

**Reform priorities (low-regret fixes):**
1. **Documentation & notice.** Standardize what gets recorded (basis, scope, duration, appeal) and what affected parties must be told, to reduce arbitrary leverage  [C067], [C072].
2. **Use procurement levers as procurement levers.** Prefer explicit contracting restrictions/conditions over “designation” signals that invite ambiguous chilling effects  [C050], [C086], [C087].
3. **Rebuild resilience regardless of who ‘wins’.** If dependency is even arguably present, invest in redundancy and vendor portability  [C026], [C081], [C085].
