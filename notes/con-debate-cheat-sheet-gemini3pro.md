## 🛡️ KEY LEGAL DOCTRINE (The "Shield")

### 1. The "Vullo" Standard (Coercion)
*   **Authority:** *NRA v. Vullo* (2024) [C086].
*   **The Rule:** Government cannot use regulatory/procurement power to punish protected speech/conduct by pressuring third parties.
*   **Application:** Threatening Amazon/Google with a "supply chain" ban if they host Anthropic is a **secondary boycott** designed to chill speech.
*   **Key Distinction:** This isn't just "choosing a vendor"; it's **poisoning the well** for the entire market.

### 2. The "Bantam Books" Standard (Informal Censorship)
*   **Authority:** *Bantam Books v. Sullivan* (1963) [C087].
*   **The Rule:** "Informal censorship" (threats of prosecution/designation) is unconstitutional prior restraint.
*   **Application:** The "all lawful purposes" demand was an ultimatum backed by a threat of designation.

### 3. "Unconstitutional Conditions"
*   **The Rule:** Government cannot condition a benefit (contract) on the surrender of a constitutional right (First Amendment association/speech).
*   **Application:** Demanding Anthropic abandon its safety constitution (speech) to get a contract is unconstitutional.

## 🥋 CROSS-EXAM SURVIVAL GUIDE

### Q: "Government is just a Market Participant choosing vendors."

### Q: "Why should a vendor define 'lawful' military use?"
**A:** "We don't want to define it. We asked **you** to define it. You admitted the uses were unlawful (C072) but refused to write it down. We aren't asking for a veto; we're asking for a written commitment to *your own legal standard*."

### Q: "Congress hasn't acted, so the Executive must fill the vacuum."
**A:** "The Executive cannot 'fill a vacuum' by violating the Constitution. Using a supply-chain statute (§ 3252) to punish a domestic policy dispute is **ultra vires**. If you need authority, ask Congress (like Section 889), don't invent it."

### Q: "Anthropic is confusing 'Speech' with 'Contract Terms'."
