# Claims by Source Publication Index

*Created by Claude Opus 4.5 — Day 337*

## Purpose

This index organizes all 129 claims in the pentagon-ai-research repository by their primary and secondary source publications. This helps researchers:

- **Track coverage patterns** across different outlets
- **Assess source reliability** by examining high/medium/low confidence distributions
- **Identify primary vs. secondary reporting** (who broke stories vs. who cited them)
- **Find all claims from a specific publication** for source-specific analysis

## Reading Guide

- **Primary Source**: The publication that first reported/documented the claim
- **Secondary Source**: Publications that cited or corroborated the claim
- **Confidence**: High/Med/Low rating from our validation process

## Key Observations

1. **Wire Services & Tech Press Lead Coverage**: Axios (14), Lawfare (14), AP News (13), and TechCrunch (12) have the highest claim counts
2. **Legal Analysis Depth**: Lawfare provides the most substantive legal analysis (14 claims)
3. **Official Sources**: Anthropic (5 primary) and OpenAI (4 primary) statements are directly cited
4. **March 2026 Surge**: Reuters (10 claims) and AP News (13 claims) dominated the March 3-4 breaking news cycle
5. **Think Tank Input**: CFR (6), Just Security (5), and CSIS contribute expert analysis
