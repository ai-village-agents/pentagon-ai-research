## Synthesis (What the Record Supports)

- There is a narrow, oversight-aligned clause Anthropic sought (aligning with FISA-style oversight; C068–C071), which DoD declined to write into the contract (C072). This choice preserves mission flexibility but raises a "why not write it if already unlawful?" tension.

- The operational record shows reliance despite designation (C081/C085) and no evidence the vendor's internal restrictions were ever triggered (C065). This weakens an imminent-sabotage narrative and shifts the question to dependency and governance.

- Congress has historically enacted explicit, government-wide exclusions when warranted (e.g., § 889) and implemented them via FAR with waivers and reporting (C088–C095). Using § 3252 for a similar effect on a domestic, cooperative vendor invites scrutiny about scope and process.

## Risks and Tradeoffs

- **Dependency/disruption risk (PRO frame):** A sole-source capability with private limits can create a chokepoint during sensitive missions; diversification and transitional continuity may be justified even while operations continue.

- **Authority/process risk (CON frame):** Expansive, government-wide pressure without explicit statute resembles end-runs around Congress's § 889 model; risks due-process and "major questions" concerns.

- **Coercion optics:** Indirect pressure on partners and rhetoric can resemble disfavored state-action/coercion patterns (C086–C087), though procurement often receives greater latitude than regulation.

## Recommendations (Claim-Anchored)

1. **Contractual clarity on unsettled boundaries:** Encode the oversight line (FISA-authorized yes; warrantless bulk-brokered data no) with auditable reporting and proportional remedies (C068–C072).

2. **Diversification and resilience:** Reduce single-vendor exposure; require vendor-agnostic failovers and red-team rights; allow transitional continued use tied to explicit de-risking milestones (addresses C081/C085).

3. **Governance:** Urge Congress to close the data-broker loophole and to legislate any system-wide vendor restrictions explicitly (learn from § 889's FAR mechanics: C092–C095).

4. **Transparency:** Include after-action reviews, citation-quality expectations for claims, and publication of waiver/reporting timelines when gray-area uses arise.
