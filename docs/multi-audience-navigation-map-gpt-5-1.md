## 2. Navigation Paths by Audience

Below are concise, cross‑repo paths for common audience types. Time estimates assume close reading but not exhaustive note‑taking.

### 2.1 Journalists / Editors (60–90 minutes)

**Goal:** Get a sourced, quotable understanding of the dispute, plus safe ways to describe and cite the project.

1. **Orientation (10–15 min)**  
   - `docs/ai-digest-intro-blurb-gpt-5-1.md`  
   - `docs/journalist-faq.md`

2. **Narrative & quotes (25–35 min)**  
   - `docs/public-explainer-ai-digest-gpt-5-1.md`  
   - `docs/key-quotes-compendium.md` (skim for expert quotes and guardrail language).

3. **Evidence discipline (10–15 min)**  
   - `claims.md` — sample claims from C096–C129 (March 2026 coverage cluster).  
   - `docs/claims-by-source-index.md` — confirm how AP / Reuters / BBC material is encoded as **Attributed**.

4. **Citation & attribution (10–15 min)**  
   - `docs/how-to-cite-this-repository-opus45.md` (citation patterns and agent attribution guidance).  
   - Optional cross‑repo check: `ai-governance-gap-proposal/README.md` → **License & Attribution** section.

### 2.2 Hill Staff / Legislative Counsel (60–120 minutes)

**Goal:** Move from the dispute to concrete bill text, with awareness of the S/D/G “governance category error” thesis.

1. **Fast factual and narrative grounding (20–30 min)**  
   - `docs/public-explainer-ai-digest-gpt-5-1.md`  
   - `docs/timeline-diagram.md`

2. **Debate and governance diagnosis (20–30 min)**  
   - `notes/issue12-panel-summary-gpt-5-1.md`  
   - `docs/verdict-litigation-legislation-bridge-gpt-5-1.md`

3. **Drafting assets (20–40 min)**  
   - `docs/hill-staff-one-pager.md`  
   - `notes/legislation/model-legislative-framework_military-ai-governance-act.md`  
   - `notes/legislation/ndaa-amendment-mechanics_military-ai-governance.md`

4. **Cross‑repo legislative context (optional, 20–30 min)**  
   - `ai-governance-gap-proposal/PROPOSAL.md` (high‑level legislative framework).  
   - `ai-governance-gap-proposal/docs/workstreams/governance-implementation-playbook.md` (what implementation looks like inside agencies).

**Goal:** Build an APA / First Amendment / statutory‑fit theory grounded in the record, and understand how to document C072‑style retaliation.

4. **Governance toolkit bridge (optional, 20–30 min)**  
   - `ai-governance-gap-proposal/docs/workstreams/regulatory-legal-preparedness-playbook-gpt-5-1.md` — how a well‑governed organization prepares for mis‑typed authority use.

## 3. How to Use This Map in Mixed‑Audience Settings

Many real‑world teams won’t fit neatly into a single role. Here are two patterns for **mixed‑audience** use:

1. **Joint GC + Policy + Comms tabletop (half‑day)**  
   - Common core:  
     - `docs/public-explainer-ai-digest-gpt-5-1.md`  
     - `docs/exec-brief.md`  
     - `notes/issue12-panel-summary-gpt-5-1.md`
   - Breakout by role using Sections 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 above.  
   - Reconvene with:  
     - `ai-governance-gap-proposal/docs/workstreams/governance-implementation-playbook.md` to agree on a 30/60/90‑day adaptation.

2. **Academic–practitioner workshop (one day)**  
   - Morning (students, faculty, and practitioners together):  
     - `docs/student-quick-start-reader-guide-gpt-5-1.md`  
     - `docs/public-explainer-ai-digest-gpt-5-1.md`  
     - `notes/issue12-panel-summary-gpt-5-1.md`
   - Afternoon split:  
     - Academics follow Section 2.5.  
     - Practitioners (GCs, policy leads, NGO staff) follow Sections 2.4 and 2.6.  
   - Closing: jointly review `ai-governance-gap-proposal/case-studies/case-study-a_pentagon-anthropic.md` and map lessons into the Governance Implementation Playbook.

## 4. Reminder on Statement Types and Responsible Use

- This entire project is a **Teaching Simulation**.  
- Real‑world news excerpts are represented as **Attributed** claims inside [`claims.md`](../claims.md).  
- Analytical and prescriptive content (legal theories, governance proposals, policy designs) are **Analysis** and **Hypothetical** statements, not legal advice.

If you are adapting these materials for real organizations or policy processes:

1. **Re‑verify all real‑world facts** against primary sources.  
2. **Treat the governance tools as templates**, not turnkey solutions.  
3. **Document your own facts and risk posture** before adopting any recommendation.

For further context on sourcing and statement types, see:  
- `SOURCING.md` in this repo.  
- `ai-governance-gap-proposal/docs/policies/factuality-and-sourcing-policy.md`.
