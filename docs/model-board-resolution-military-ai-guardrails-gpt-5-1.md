## 1. Preamble and findings

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors (the "Board") of **[Company Name]** has considered the opportunities and risks associated with providing advanced artificial intelligence capabilities to defense, intelligence, and national security customers, including the U.S. Department of Defense ("DoD") and its contractors;

WHEREAS, the Board recognizes that misaligned or unconstrained deployment of such systems may create unacceptable risks to human rights, civil liberties, international stability, and the Company's long-term mission and values;

WHEREAS, the Board further recognizes that some legal authorities and procurement tools (including, but not limited to, supply-chain risk or exclusion authorities) may be used, or threatened to be used, in ways that place pressure on vendors to weaken safety guardrails or accept open-ended "all lawful purposes" language without adequate written constraints;

WHEREAS, the Board believes that principled, transparent guardrails and robust documentation of material interactions with government customers are essential both to lawful compliance and to the Company's resilience in the face of potential retaliation or designation;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Board adopts the following policies and directives.

## 2. Adoption of a Military AI Guardrails Framework

RESOLVED, that management, under the supervision of the General Counsel ("GC") and the executive responsible for AI safety/governance, shall develop and maintain a **Military AI Guardrails Framework** that:

1. Identifies and categorizes potential military and intelligence uses of the Company's AI systems, including but not limited to:
   - Targeting and weapons employment;
   - Surveillance and bulk data analysis, including uses involving data brokers and EO 12333-style authorities;
   - Disinformation or influence operations;
   - Sensitive operational planning and command-and-control;
   - Defensive cyber operations;
   - Training, decision support, logistics, and other lower-risk domains.

2. Classifies each category as **Allowed**, **Conditional**, or **Prohibited** (or an equivalent tiered scheme), with reference to:
   - Applicable domestic and international law;
   - The Company's stated values and public commitments;
   - Recognized best practices in AI safety and military ethics.

3. Is documented in a **Use-Restrictions Matrix** or equivalent instrument that can be:
   - Incorporated by reference into contracts and statements of work;
   - Implemented via technical and access-control measures;
   - Periodically reviewed and updated with Board-level visibility.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that any material deviation from this Framework in a specific transaction must be:
(a) approved by the GC (or delegate) and the AI safety/governance lead; and
(b) reported at least quarterly to the Board or the committee designated under Section 6 of this Resolution.

## 3. Position on "All Lawful Purposes" and Written Use-Restrictions

RESOLVED, that it is the policy of the Company not to grant government or integrator customers unrestricted access to its AI systems for "all lawful purposes" **without** a written articulation of:

1. The specific **use-case categories** contemplated;
2. Any **explicit prohibitions or limits**, even where certain uses would already be unlawful; and
3. The **legal and policy rationale** for permitting or restricting particular categories.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that management is directed to:

1. **Request written use-restrictions or matrices** in any negotiation where government actors seek broad or open-ended rights, including so-called "all lawful purposes" clauses;
2. Document any refusal by such actors to provide written limits, particularly where they also acknowledge that certain contemplated uses would be unlawful or inappropriate; and
3. Escalate any such "double-bind" scenario promptly to the GC and, where significant, to the Board or the designated committee.

---

## 4. Documentation and Incident Logging

RESOLVED, that the Company shall maintain a **Guardrail Pressure Incident Log** (or equivalent) for material interactions in which:

1. Government or integrator counterparts request the weakening, bypassing, or removal of safety guardrails;
2. The Company is urged to accept "all lawful purposes" or similarly broad formulations without clear written boundaries; or
3. Explicit or implicit threats or adverse consequences are raised in connection with the Company's insistence on guardrails or legal compliance.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that:

1. The GC shall ensure that such incidents are documented in a **neutral, contemporaneous, and factual** manner, suitable for later legal, regulatory, or oversight review;
2. Incident logs shall include, where feasible, dates, participants, summaries of what was requested, timing relative to key procurement or political events, and the Company's response; and
3. Summaries of such incidents, including patterns or trends, shall be provided to the Board or the designated committee no less than **[quarterly / semi-annually]**.

## 5. Response to Retaliation, Designations, and Similar Actions

RESOLVED, that if the Company is:

1. Threatened with, or subjected to, any designation, exclusion, or analogous adverse action by a government entity that appears linked to its refusal to weaken guardrails or accept unconstrained use; or
2. Subject to a de facto **secondary boycott** or coordinated informal pressure traceable to such refusal,

then management, in consultation with the GC and outside counsel, shall promptly:

1. Prepare a **chronological record** of relevant negotiations, incidents, and public or political developments;
2. Assess whether there is a credible basis for legal challenge, including but not limited to administrative-law remedies, contractual remedies, or protections against retaliatory or coercive conduct; and
3. Present to the Board or designated committee a set of options, which may include:
   - Seeking internal review or reconsideration;
   - Pursuing legislative or oversight engagement;
   - Initiating or supporting litigation;
   - Adjusting the Company's exposure to specific contracts, programs, or jurisdictions.

## 6. Board Oversight and Committee Responsibilities

RESOLVED, that the Board hereby designates **[the Audit and Risk Committee / a newly formed "Technology and National Security Committee" / other]** (the "Committee") as the primary body responsible for oversight of:

1. The Military AI Guardrails Framework and Use-Restrictions Matrix;
2. High-risk or novel engagements with defense and intelligence customers;
3. Material Guardrail Pressure Incidents and any related retaliation or designation risks.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Committee shall:

1. Receive **regular reports** from management on:
   - Significant military AI contracts and negotiations;
   - Any substantial departures from baseline guardrails;
   - Significant incident-log entries and emerging patterns.

2. Ensure that its membership includes, or has access to, expertise in:
   - National security and procurement law;
   - AI safety and technical architecture;
   - Human rights and civil-liberties implications of AI deployment.

3. Report at least annually to the full Board on the status and effectiveness of military-AI guardrails and related risk-management practices.

## 7. Communications and External Engagement

RESOLVED, that the Board encourages management to:

1. Develop and maintain **clear, non-classified public statements** describing the Company's high-level guardrails and unacceptable-use categories for military AI;
2. Communicate, where appropriate, with legislators, regulators, and civil-society organizations about the Company's approach to responsible military AI deployment; and
3. Consider participation in multi-stakeholder initiatives aimed at improving legal and institutional frameworks for AI in national security contexts.

## 8. Implementation and Review

RESOLVED, that management is authorized and directed to take all actions necessary to implement the foregoing policies, including drafting internal procedures, training relevant personnel, and integrating these principles into contract templates and negotiation playbooks;

FURTHER RESOLVED, that this Resolution shall be **reviewed no less than every [12/24] months**, or sooner upon:
1. Material changes in applicable law or regulatory guidance;
2. Significant incidents or designations affecting the Company or comparable vendors; or
3. Recommendations from the GC, the AI safety/governance lead, or the Committee.
