## THE ISSUE

Repository documents reference TWO different section numbering schemes for the four gap provisions:

### **Scheme 1: Standalone Military AI Governance Act**
- § 201 — Dependency-Risk Authority
- § 301 — Anti-Retaliation Safeguard  
- § 302 — Classification Safeguard
- § 303 — Vendor Standing (Private Right of Action)
- § 401 — Enforcement Mechanism
- § 501 — Transition Authority

### **Scheme 2: NDAA Amendment (10 U.S.C. § 2304(f))**
- 10 U.S.C. § 2304(f)(8) — Enforcement Mechanism (auto-stay, 60-day expiry, Congressional notification)
- 10 U.S.C. § 2304(f)(9) — Transition Authority (auto-vacate legacy designations, 120-day re-determination)
- 10 U.S.C. § 2304(f) subsections — Classification safeguards, vendor standing, anti-retaliation

---

## WHY TWO SCHEMES?

**Strategic Flexibility:** Congress may act via either vehicle:

1. **NDAA Amendment (Faster, FY2026–2027 cycle)**
   - Subsections added to existing 10 U.S.C. § 2304 (procurement safeguards)
   - Narrower scope; fits existing statutory structure
   - Placement: § 2304(f)(8)–(9) with supporting subsections
   - Timeline: Could pass in next NDAA (FY2027, likely 2026 debate)

2. **Standalone Bill (Comprehensive, 2–3 cycle horizon)**
   - Full military AI governance framework
   - Broader authority; new statutory title
   - Placement: §§ 201–501 (five titles)
   - Timeline: Longer legislative lift; requires bipartisan foundation-building

**Talking Points:**

1. **Immediate Fix (NDAA, FY2027):** 
   - Add § 2304(f)(8)–(9) to next NDAA
   - Requires written restrictions on AI deployment
   - Auto-stay on controversial designations, 60-day review clock
   - Congressional notification within 48 hours

2. **Long-Term Fix (Standalone Bill, 2–3 cycles):**
   - Comprehensive Military AI Governance Act
   - Establishes dependency-risk authority (§ 201)
   - Vendor protections (§ 303), classification safeguards (§ 302), anti-retaliation (§ 301)
   - Creates predictable governance framework

3. **Why Both?**
   - NDAA fix is faster and more targeted
   - Standalone bill is more durable and comprehensive
   - Congress can do NDAA first, then long-term bill simultaneously
