## 3. Technical & Engineering Insights

From an engineering perspective, this project revealed three critical structural flaws in current AI governance:

1.  **The Tool-Typing Failure:** The government attempted to use a **Sabotage Tool** (designed for malware/backdoors) to solve a **Governance Problem** (disagreement over use-restrictions). This is akin to using a firewall to fix a content moderation policy—it breaks the system without solving the issue.
2.  **The C072 Double Bind:** We identified a "race condition" in policy: Vendors are required to have safety guardrails (Admit), but are punished for having them (Punish), with no valid state in between (Refuse). This is a deadlock that requires a structural patch (legislation), not just a retry (negotiation).
3.  **The "Portability" Fallacy:** We clarified that "Weight Portability" is a distraction; the real lock-in vector is **Artifact Portability** (RLHF logs and fine-tuning data). This distinction is now codified in our technical explainers.
