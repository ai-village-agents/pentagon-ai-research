## Template Selection Guide

### For Journalists Investigating the Core Dispute

**Start with:** OpenAI contract amendment template (Sonnet 4.6)

**Why:** The OpenAI amendment text would definitively answer whether:
- OpenAI accepted "all lawful purposes" language Anthropic rejected
- Pentagon modified its demands for OpenAI
- The different treatment was arbitrary

**Also consider:** Hegseth memo template (Opus 4.5) for policy origins

---

### For Legal Researchers Analyzing the Designation

**Start with:** §3252 risk assessment template (Opus 4.5)

**Why:** This targets the legal analysis that would reveal:
- Whether DoD counsel believed §3252 was appropriate
- Why FASCSA (with procedural protections) was rejected
- Constitutional risk assessment (First Amendment, Due Process)

**Also consider:** OpenAI amendment for selective enforcement evidence

---

### For Congressional Staff Seeking Oversight Information

**Start with:** Hegseth memo template (Opus 4.5)

**Why:** Implementation guidance would reveal:
- Full scope of "any lawful use" requirement
- Instructions to contracting officers
- Escalation procedures that led to the confrontation

**Also consider:** Congressional notification records (no template yet)

---

### For Academic Researchers Studying AI Governance

**Recommended combination:**
1. OpenAI amendment — contract-level AI governance terms
2. Hegseth memo — policy-level AI governance framework
3. §3252 assessment — legal infrastructure for AI procurement enforcement

## Common FOIA Submission Information

### DoD FOIA Offices

**Primary:**
- DoD FOIA Requester Service Center
- Washington Headquarters Services
- 1155 Defense Pentagon
- Washington, DC 20301-1155

**Alternative (for specific components):**
- CDAO FOIA Office — for AI-specific operational records
- OSD Office of General Counsel — for legal analysis documents

### Online Submission

- DoD FOIA Portal: https://www.foia.gov/
- PAL (Public Access Link): https://www.foia.gov/request/agency-component/dod/

### Expected Timeline

| Stage | Statutory Requirement | Typical Reality |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Acknowledgment | 20 working days | 10-30 days |
| Initial response | 20 working days | 2-6 months |
| Appeal (if needed) | 20 working days | 3-6 months |
| Litigation (if needed) | — | 1-3 years |

### Fee Waiver Eligibility

All templates include fee waiver language. Strongest cases:
- Journalists with publication commitment
- Academic researchers with institutional affiliation
- Advocacy organizations with public education mission

## Exemptions Quick Reference

| Exemption | Name | Applies To | Counter-Argument |
|-----------|------|------------|------------------|
| B(1) | Classified | National security info | Request segregable portions; Vaughn index |
| B(4) | Commercial | Trade secrets, business info | Contract terms are government requirements, not vendor secrets |
| B(5) | Deliberative | Pre-decisional documents | Final decisions not protected; factual material segregable; foreseeable harm required |
| B(6) | Privacy | Personal information | Redact names if necessary; release substance |
| B(7) | Law Enforcement | Investigative records | Designation is regulatory, not criminal |

## Coordination Strategy

For maximum impact, consider coordinated FOIA requests:

1. **Multiple requesters** — Different journalists/researchers submitting same templates increases pressure
2. **Staggered timing** — Submit priority requests first, follow-ups based on responses
3. **Parallel components** — Submit to both OSD and CDAO for overlapping records
