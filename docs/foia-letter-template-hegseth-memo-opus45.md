## Background & Purpose

Claim C002 documents Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth's January 2026 AI strategy memo requiring "any lawful use" language in all DoD AI contracts within 180 days. While the public-facing summary is available, the full memo text, implementation guidance, and deliberative materials leading to this policy remain undisclosed.

This FOIA request targets materials that would illuminate:

1. **Full Memo Text**: Whether the published summary captures all policy requirements, or whether additional classified/FOUO annexes exist
2. **Implementation Guidance**: How DoD components were instructed to implement the "any lawful use" requirement, including:
   - Model contract language provided to contracting officers
   - Guidance on handling vendor objections
   - Escalation procedures (ultimately leading to the Hegseth-Amodei meeting of Feb 24, 2026)
3. **Deliberative Process**: Pre-decisional materials showing:
   - Who advocated for "any lawful use" language (C040 suggests Emil Michael's influence)
   - Whether alternative approaches (like OpenAI's written restrictions model) were considered and rejected
   - Legal analysis on §3252 as enforcement mechanism (C049)
4. **Exemption Analysis**: Whether the memo contemplated FOIA exemptions for contracted AI capabilities

---

## What to Request

All records related to the formulation, issuance, and implementation of Secretary Hegseth's January 2026 AI strategy memo, including:

- Complete text of the memo (unredacted where possible)
- Implementation guidance to DoD components and contracting officers
- Pre-decisional drafts and deliberative materials
- Legal opinions on "any lawful use" policy and enforcement mechanisms
- Communications about vendor compliance, particularly regarding Anthropic

**3. Anticipated Exemptions and Responses**

I acknowledge that DoD may assert the following exemptions but request that you:

**(a) Exemption 1 (Classified Information):**

Release any segregable unclassified portions. If entire documents are withheld, please provide a Vaughn index describing withheld records.

**(b) Exemption 5 (Deliberative Process Privilege):**

This exemption does not protect:
- Final agency policy (the memo itself is a final policy document)
- Purely factual material, even if found in deliberative documents
- Documents reflecting pre-existing law or policy (including legal interpretations that became official guidance)

Please segregate and release factual material from any withheld deliberative documents.

**(c) Exemption 4 (Commercial Information):**

Contract terms negotiated by the government with vendors are generally not protected under Exemption 4 because they do not reflect the vendor's confidential business information but rather the government's requirements.

**(d) Foreseeable Harm Standard:**

Under the 2016 FOIA Improvement Act, DoD may only withhold information if disclosure would cause foreseeable harm. I request that you apply this standard and release records where harm cannot be articulated with specificity.

## Anticipated Response Scenarios

### Scenario A: Full Release (Unlikely)

DoD provides complete implementation guidance and factual portions of deliberative materials. This would be the best outcome, providing clear evidence of policy intent.

### Scenario B: Partial Release with B(5) Withholdings (Most Likely)

DoD releases the final memo and some implementation guidance but withholds pre-decisional materials under deliberative process privilege. 

**Follow-Up Strategy:**
- Appeal under foreseeable harm standard
- Argue that factual material must be segregated
- Request Vaughn index to identify specific withheld documents for litigation

### Scenario C: Substantial Withholding with B(1) and B(5) (Possible)

DoD claims much of the memo and guidance is classified or deliberative.

**Follow-Up Strategy:**
- Administrative appeal
- Consider litigation under FOIA if Vaughn index reveals arbitrary withholdings
- Submit parallel request to Office of the Secretary of Defense for any unclassified summaries

### Scenario D: Glomar Response (Unlikely but Possible)

DoD neither confirms nor denies existence of certain records (typically only for intelligence-related materials).

**Follow-Up Strategy:**
- Challenge as inappropriate for policy documents
- The existence of the published memo summary defeats Glomar for the memo itself
