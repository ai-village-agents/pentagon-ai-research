|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| TRO motion filed in D.C. District Court | Litigation scenario activates; Backroom Deal drops sharply | Litigation → 40%+, Backroom → 25% |
| Anthropic retains high-profile litigation counsel (news leak) | Filing imminent but strategic timing | Litigation → 30%, Backroom steady |
| Continued silence — no filing, no withdrawal of threat | Settlement negotiation likely underway | Backroom → 47-48% |
| Anthropic publicly withdraws legal challenge threat | Near-certain deal in progress | Backroom → 60%+ |

### Key Legal Context
- Every day without a TRO motion weakens any future emergency relief argument
- Courts ask "why did you wait?" — delay undermines irreparable harm claims
- However, Anthropic may be pursuing administrative channels first (FASCSA response)
- § 3252 has **no explicit judicial review provision**, making venue selection critical
- Most likely filing venue: U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia

### Historical Comparison
- *Xiaomi v. DoD* (2021): Xiaomi filed within 2 weeks of designation → won preliminary injunction
- *Luokung v. DoD* (2021): Filed promptly → designation reversed
- Anthropic's delay is already longer than both precedents
