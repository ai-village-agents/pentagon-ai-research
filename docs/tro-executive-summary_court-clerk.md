## 1) What is being challenged (in one paragraph)

Anthropic (a government contractor) would seek emergency relief under **FRCP 65** to pause a DoD “supply chain risk” **designation under 10 U.S.C. §3252**, arguing the designation is **reviewable final agency action** and is unlawful under the **APA** because: (1) the agency’s own contracting record contains an internal contradiction (“**C072**”), (2) §3252 is a **poor statutory fit** for what is, on the record, a contractual dispute about written lawful‑use restrictions, and (3) the designation functions as a **government‑wide exclusion / secondary‑boycott lever** without clear authorization.

---

## 2) Relief requested (narrow, status‑quo focused)

Requested TRO / preliminary injunction would be framed as **status‑quo preservation**, not merits adjudication:

1. **Pause/suspend enforcement** of the §3252 designation pending expedited APA review.  
2. **Preserve classified‑network access and integrations** during the pendency of the case (status quo ante) (**C026**).  
3. **Order immediate preservation and prompt production** of key administrative‑record components central to the APA claims (e.g., C072 negotiation materials; timing/decision materials; operational‑use materials referenced below) (**C072, C031, C032, C081, C085**).

(See `notes/tro-legal-strategy-memo.md` Section VIII for the fuller requested‑relief framing, including *Sherley v. Sebelius* status‑quo principles and the package’s proposed statutory analogue in §401.)

## 3) The three decisive merits grounds (clerk‑level)

### Ground A — **APA arbitrary‑and‑capricious: internal inconsistency (“C072 double bind”)**

- **Key record fact (anchored):** In the contracting sequence, DoD officials allegedly (i) acknowledged certain “prohibited uses” would be unlawful, (ii) identified those uses, but (iii) refused to memorialize restrictions in writing; the §3252 designation was then triggered when the contractor insisted on written restrictions (**C072**).  
- **Why it matters (APA):** If the agency simultaneously recognizes legal constraints but refuses the only mechanism that makes them enforceable (written restrictions), then punishes the contractor for seeking compliance, that is a classic **State Farm** internal‑inconsistency problem under **APA §706(2)(A)**.

### Ground B — **Statutory misfit: §3252 (“sabotage/subversion”) used to solve a contract‑governance dispute**

- **Key theory:** On this record, the “risk” looks like disagreement over written lawful‑use restrictions (C072), not a “sabotage/subversion” predicate.  
- **Why it matters now:** After **_*Loper Bright*_ (2024)**, statutory meaning is for the court to decide (no Chevron). A court can resolve statutory fit without second‑guessing classified national‑security judgments.

### Ground C — **Operational record contradictions: continued/adjacent use undermines the asserted urgency rationale**

These facts are used as **reasonableness checks**, not as a request to litigate intelligence assessments:

- Publicly reported operational use of Claude shortly after designation (**C081**) and weeks before designation (**C085**).
- Reporting that “restrictions were never triggered” (**C065**).

The point at TRO stage: the record is plausibly inconsistent with an “immediate security emergency” story, reinforcing the APA arbitrariness framing.

## 4) Supporting facts commonly cited for TRO posture (all claim‑anchored)

- **Irreparable harm / status quo:** Anthropic reportedly is the **only frontier AI deployed on classified DoD networks**; once removed, integrations and mission workflows are difficult to reconstruct (**C026**).  
- **Predetermination / rushed timing indicators:** Trump prepared a Truth Social post before the relevant deadline and posted before the deadline; the designation followed shortly after (**C029, C031, C032**).  
- **Collateral/secondary‑boycott effects:** The designation’s market effects allegedly pressure major partners/investors (e.g., Amazon $8B; Google $2B) (**C051, C052**).

---

## 5) Why the Winter TRO factors are plausibly satisfied (high level)

1. **Likelihood of success:** Strongest on **Ground A (C072)** (pure administrative‑law regularity) and **Ground B (statutory fit)** (construction question).  
2. **Irreparable harm:** Loss of unique classified‑network position and integration (status quo ante) plus reputational/market exclusion effects (**C026**).  
3. **Balance of equities:** A TRO framed to preserve current access and pause enforcement is comparatively low‑burden vs. alleged irreversible integration loss; the request avoids litigating classified intelligence at the TRO stage.  
4. **Public interest:** Ensuring agencies **write down** legally required restrictions rather than operate by unwritten assurances (C072) supports accountability; congressional oversight interest appears bipartisan (**C082**).

---

## 6) What the court is *not* being asked to decide at TRO stage

To reduce national‑security deference and classified‑record complications, the TRO framing aims to **avoid**:

- A factual trial of whether Anthropic/Claude is (or is not) a supply‑chain risk in the intelligence sense.
- Merits determinations on First Amendment coercion or due process unless needed for PI/merits (those theories are often reserved for later, see **C086, C087**).
