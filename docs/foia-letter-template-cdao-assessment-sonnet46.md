## Background: Why This Record Matters

On February 27, 2026 (5:14 PM ET), Secretary Hegseth designated Anthropic as a "Supply-Chain Risk" under 10 U.S.C. § 3252. The Hegseth designation presupposes that the Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office (CDAO) assessed Claude as posing unacceptable risk to DoD operations.

Yet publicly available evidence cuts sharply against any such finding:

- **C065:** The safety restrictions Anthropic refused to waive had *never* been triggered in operational use
- **C081:** Claude was used in DoD classified systems within 5 days of the designation — suggesting ongoing operational value, not risk
- **C085:** A Venezuela operation used Claude *weeks before* the designation
- **C108:** ~1,000 targets in 24 hours during the Iran operation — enabled by or alongside Claude

If the CDAO conducted a genuine technical risk assessment, its contents could confirm or refute the legal basis for the designation. If no such assessment exists, the designation may lack the factual foundation required under the Administrative Procedure Act (5 U.S.C. § 706(2)(A)) — the arbitrary-and-capricious standard.

This template is designed for journalists, researchers, legal teams, and public interest advocates seeking that CDAO assessment record.

## Legal Framework: Why CDAO Records Are Critical

### APA Arbitrary-and-Capricious Standard

Under *Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm* (1983) and subsequent D.C. Circuit precedent, an agency action is arbitrary and capricious if the agency:

1. **Relied on factors Congress did not intend it to consider** (e.g., CEO personal animus)
2. **Entirely failed to consider an important aspect of the problem** (e.g., whether restrictions were ever triggered)
3. **Offered an explanation for the decision that runs counter to the evidence before the agency** (e.g., designating a system currently in active operational use)
4. **Made a decision so implausible it cannot be ascribed to a difference in view or product of agency expertise**

CDAO assessment records would be central to any APA challenge because:

- If CDAO **found Claude safe** → the designation was made against CDAO's expert recommendation → factor (1) or (3) above
- If CDAO **found Claude risky** → the factual basis of that finding is subject to review → factor (3)
- If **no assessment was conducted** → the designation lacked reasoned basis → factor (2)

### 10 U.S.C. § 3252 Statutory Fit

As analyzed in `notes/c019-section-3252-statutory-text.md` and `notes/c034-section-3252-implementing-regs.md`, § 3252 was drafted to address adversarial foreign-government sabotage of supply chains (Huawei/ZTE-era legislation), not commercial contract disagreements with domestic AI companies. Any CDAO assessment that characterizes Anthropic's safety guidelines as "supply chain sabotage" would be subject to challenge on statutory grounds.

## Anticipated Exemption Claims and Counter-Arguments

| Exemption | Likely Claim | Counter-Argument |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Exemption 1** (Classified) | Assessment contains classified operational details | Request segregable unclassified portions; request Vaughn index of classified redactions |
| **Exemption 5** (Deliberative Process) | Assessment is pre-decisional deliberative | Exception: final decision was announced publicly (Feb 27 designation); segregable factual portions not protected |
| **Exemption 5** (Attorney-Client) | Legal analysis prepared at counsel's direction | Request factual (non-legal) portions; legal conclusions in final agency action often segregable |
| **Exemption 3** (Statutory) | Protected by statute (e.g., 50 U.S.C. § 3024) | Identify specific statute claimed; challenge overly broad assertion |

### The "Foreseeable Harm" Standard (FOIA Improvement Act 2016)

Under 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(8)(A)(i), agencies must now demonstrate "foreseeable harm" from disclosure before withholding records — not merely that a record falls within an exemption category. A CDAO technical risk assessment of a commercial AI company that was *already designated publicly* presents a weak foreseeable-harm argument.

## Key Terms and Definitions

**CDAO:** Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office — DoD's lead office for AI integration. Established 2022, successor to JAIC (Joint Artificial Intelligence Center). Responsible for AI policy, standards, and operational deployment across DoD.

**§ 3252:** 10 U.S.C. § 3252 — "Prohibition on procurement of telecommunications equipment or services from certain entities" — the Cold War-era supply-chain statute repurposed for the Anthropic designation. Originally designed for Huawei/ZTE-type threats.

**Supply-Chain Risk:** Under § 3252, a finding that a vendor's product or service poses unacceptable risk due to potential compromise by a foreign adversary. No prior domestic U.S. company has been designated under this authority.

**APA § 706:** Administrative Procedure Act's judicial review standard — courts shall set aside agency action that is "arbitrary, capricious, an abuse of discretion, or otherwise not in accordance with law."
